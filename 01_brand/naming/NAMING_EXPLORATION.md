# Naming — Parallax Playbook

**Final name:** Parallax Playbook
**Decided:** April 2026

---

## The meaning

**Parallax** — the shift in perception that happens when you change viewpoints. The gap between how something looks from one angle and how it actually is from another. In trading: the gap between what you think you're doing and what you're actually doing. The gap between the trader you think you are and the trader you actually are. The core psychological thesis of the site.

**Playbook** — what you do about it. The record of your thinking, your methods, your honest work. A living document, not a frozen rulebook.

Together: **Parallax Playbook** — the shift in perception, and what you do with it. Perception change met with disciplined action.

---

## Why this name won

After extensive exploration (40+ candidates across multiple rounds), Parallax Playbook was chosen despite known tradeoffs. The decision was made consciously, with eyes open.

**What we got:**
- Meaningful on both words
- Memorable and catchy
- Brandable — both words have identity and weight
- Two words, similar length, good rhythm
- Passes the "I use Parallax Playbook" sentence test
- The alliteration works
- Conceptually tight — the name literally describes the project

**What we accepted:**
- "Parallax" is crowded in fintech (Parallax Labs, Parallax.markets, Parallax Volatility Advisers, Parallax Financial Research, Parallax Finance). The compound "Parallax Playbook" is not directly taken, but the surname is loaded.
- SEO will be a permanent headwind — searches for "Parallax" surface other fintech entities first.
- At scale, there's a nonzero risk of a cease-and-desist from a larger Parallax entity in the trademark class (financial services). Manageable at indie scale; something to monitor if the site grows significantly.
- The name is slightly long. Acceptable.

**Why the tradeoffs are fine:**
- The site is not optimizing for SEO. It's optimizing for being a place serious readers return to.
- Legal risk is low at current scale and only materializes if the project succeeds enough to be noticed. "Having to rebrand at scale" is a good problem to have.
- The emotional resonance of the name outweighs the competitive friction.

## The decision log

For reference, the full exploration that led here is below.

### The brief the name had to satisfy

1. Rolls off the tongue — passes the "I use ___" sentence test
2. Grounded, not mystical — real word(s) or real-word-feeling
3. Has meaning — encodes the meta-feature or captures the philosophy
4. Realistic — sounds like a real independent project/company
5. A bit boring is fine — doesn't strain for effect
6. Easy in a sentence
7. Clean enough namespace — not directly conflicting with an active trademark

Parallax Playbook passes 1-6 clearly. On 7, the compound is clean but the surname is loaded — accepted.

### Candidates that also survived DD (for reference only)

If the name ever needed to be reconsidered, these were the other survivors that passed real due diligence:

- **Print Lab** — "Print" = trade execution on the tape. Clean. Minor SEO noise from 3D printing education platforms.
- **Fill Lab** — "Fill" = order execution. Clean. Double-L slightly awkward in URL.
- **Setup Lab** — "Setup" = tradeable pattern. Clean but somewhat generic.
- **Range Lab** — "Range" = daily price range. Clean. Dual-meaning.
- **Scalp Desk** — Clean namespace, but "scalp" has baggage.
- **Markout** — Trader term. Clean. Felt boring at the time; still a real option.
- **Bearings** — Clean. Immediate meaning.
- **Rundown** — Short, active, trader-adjacent. Clean.
- **Ground Truth** — Philosophically exact. Slightly academic.

### Candidates eliminated by DD (do not revisit)

- **Parallax** (standalone) — too crowded in fintech
- **Meridian, Azimuth, Lighthouse, Sextant, Trailhead, Throughline, Ballast, Compass, Horizon, Fathom, Apogee, Tessera, Veridian, Solmira, Postmark, Longshot, Trade Desk, Edge Lab** — various trademark / saturation issues
- **"Tape" family** (Tape Desk, Tape House, Tape Press, Tape Lab, etc.) — user preference, not liked
- **"Keelson" and nautical-obscure words** — too insider
- **Mystical / invented fantasy names** (Halcyra, Vellari, Orinax type) — too hype
- **Hype-y vocabulary** (ninja, guru, alpha, etc.) — wrong for the brand

## When the name gets second-guessed later

It will. Every creator second-guesses their name at some point. A few reminders for future-you:

- The name was chosen with full awareness of the tradeoffs. Don't re-litigate.
- SEO headwinds are a feature, not a bug — they force the site to grow by reputation instead of search, which is a stronger foundation.
- Rebranding is always an option if the project genuinely outgrows the name. It's a cost, not a catastrophe.
- If the doubt is strong and persistent for more than a few months, that's real signal. If it's a bad week of self-doubt, ignore it.

The name is not the product. The writing, the videos, the experiments — those are the product. Parallax Playbook is just what we call the place they live.
