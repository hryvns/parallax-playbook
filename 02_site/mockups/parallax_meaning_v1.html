I

The word, before it meant anything.

From Greek parallaxis — alteration. The shift. The change of place. A word coined for a problem astronomers had not yet solved: how do you measure the distance to a star you cannot reach?

You wait. You let the Earth carry you. In January, you mark a star's position against the void. Six months later — the planet has swung 300 million kilometers across the sun — you measure again. The star has moved. Not really; it is too far for that. You have moved, and the star, seen now from a different angle, betrays its distance through how much it appears to shift.

The shift is tiny. For our nearest stellar neighbor, less than one second of arc — an angle so small you could cover it with a pinhead held at arm's length. From this near-nothing, we measured the size of the universe.

The gap between two views, applied honestly, became distance itself.

III

Same data. Two minds.

The sky has parallax because observers stand in different places. So do minds. Below, identical price action — the exact same candles, drawn from the same numbers — read by two traders. The bars don't move. The story does.

↑ BULL'S READ
Higher low. Reload zone.
Sees a textbook retest. Demand defended the prior breakout. Volume confirms accumulation. The dip is opportunity, the structure is intact, the trend has not been broken — only tested. Position size: full.
↓ BEAR'S READ
Lower high. Distribution.
Sees a failed breakout, momentum waning, each push weaker than the last. The supposed support is just where late longs are trapped. The bounce is exit liquidity. Position size: short, scaling.
Same forty-eight bars. Same volume. Same close.
Neither chart contains its own interpretation.
Every price you have ever looked at was a question your prior beliefs answered for you.

V

The market is a parallax engine.

Every trade requires disagreement. A buyer who believes price will rise meets a seller who believes it will fall. The price prints because they disagree. The market is not a thing you observe — it is the moving record of the gap between two views.

Bull line
Rising support. Each dip held. Belief: trend is intact, price is on sale.
Bear line
Falling resistance. Each rally rejected. Belief: trend is exhausted, price is distribution.
The apex
Where the two views can no longer coexist. Volatility collapses, then breaks one way. The parallax resolves into a direction.

The novice trader looks at a chart and asks: is it going up or down? — as if the chart itself contained the answer.

The serious trader holds two charts in their head simultaneously. They ask: where am I standing, and what am I refusing to see from here? They learn to walk around their own conviction, look at it from the opposite side of the orbit, and measure the angle.

Edge is not in being right. Edge is in occupying enough vantage points to see depth where others see flatness. The gap between your view and the consensus is not noise. It is the only place a trade can live.

VI · the practice

Hold a position. Then walk to the other side of your own orbit.

Look back at it from there.

The gap is the trade.
